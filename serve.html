1. The High Badminton Serve This type of serve is usually executed when you want the shuttle to land at the back end of the court. A good high serve must have the shuttle dropping steeply downwards at the back end of the court. 2.The Low Badminton Serve The low serve is used when you want the shuttlecock to land in front of the court (in front of your opponent). A GOOD low serve will have the shuttlecock flying JUST ABOVE THE NET. 3.The Flick Serve (“Fake” Low Serve) The “fake” low badminton serve can also be used to trick your opponent (deceive your opponent to expect a low serve).
